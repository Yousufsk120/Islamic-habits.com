# Islamic Habits Design System

## Brand Intent

Islamic Habits should feel like a future-facing Islamic practice civilization: spiritually warm, premium, disciplined, and useful. The design should help people move from awe to action: Salah on time, dua, dhikr, family service, knowledge, and Ummah-building.

## Audience

- Young Muslims who need energy, clarity, and beauty.
- Families who want practical Islamic routines.
- People beginning Islamic practice and seekers who need gentle guidance.
- Readers interested in Islamic history, philosophy, and civilization.

## Visual Direction

- Mood: spiritual, cinematic, calm, premium, futuristic.
- Avoid generic landing-page decoration. Every section should feel functional or meaningful.
- Use cosmic Islamic imagery carefully: creation, signs, light, time, direction, and reflection.
- Keep the first screen powerful, not crowded.
- Use cards for individual repeated items and tools, not for every section.

## Color Roles

- Deep background: `#08110f`, `#061713`, `#0d1c19`
- Primary practice green: `#42d19b`
- Qibla / knowledge aqua: `#5bd6e8`
- Noor / scholarship gold: `#f0bf55`
- Text: `#f6f2e8`
- Muted text: `#a9b9b3`
- Lines: `rgba(246, 242, 232, 0.14)`

## Typography

- Use strong, readable sans-serif typography.
- Headings should be large only in hero or major editorial sections.
- Tool panels, product cards, and blog cards should use tighter headings.
- Letter spacing should be normal except small uppercase eyebrow labels.

## Core Components

- Hero: full-bleed visual atmosphere with clear practice action.
- Practice tools: dense but calm panels for Salah time, Qibla, and practical actions.
- Blog cards: editorial, image-led, easy to scan.
- Product catalogue: tactile, trustworthy, preorder-friendly.
- Civilization layers: five clickable layers: Soul, Self, Family, Ummah, Civilization.
- Article pages: immersive reading with figures, captions, bilingual content, and PDF reader blocks.

## Homepage Structure

- First viewport: Islamic Habits identity, cosmic/noor atmosphere, direct calls to Salah/Qibla, dua practice, and the daily blog.
- World tools: location-first Salah time, Qibla direction, and a calm explanation of how the user can act today.
- 100 Hours Dua Practice: launch edition should feel generous, premium, and readable, with free download while launch access is free.
- Daily blog: make it feel like a living magazine for Islamic practice, selfhood, civilization, and useful habits.
- Product catalogue: Miswak, prayer cap, prayer mat, tasbih, books, and future habit-building bundles. Make products feel physical, warm, and trustworthy.
- Civilization layers: five entry points that open deeper reflection, each with image, short meaning, daily practice, and source-led wisdom.

## Blog Article Structure

- Keep article pages mobile-first and readable.
- Each article should have a strong opening image, a table of topics, compact bilingual sections when needed, and a recommended reading block with links.
- Use figures to teach, not decorate. Captions should explain why the image matters.
- PDF source/readers should appear after the article body so readers can continue inside the website.

## Product Section Direction

- The product section should not look like random ecommerce. It should feel like a habit catalogue.
- Each product card should show: product purpose, daily use case, who it helps, launch status, and contact/support flow.
- The tone should be sincere and practical, not aggressive sales language.

1. A cinematic Islamic future homepage.
2. A practical dense homepage optimized for tools and products.
3. A premium editorial homepage optimized for blogs and PDF reading.

After Stitch creates a direction, export or share the design/code and integrate only the strongest parts into the live site. Preserve the existing Islamic Habits content, support email, free dua launch access, and mobile readability.

## Interaction Rules

- Buttons should be obvious actions.
- Use icon-like visual cues where possible.
- Mobile navigation must remain visible on blog pages.
- Text must not overlap images or controls.
- All images need useful alt text.
- PDF/source material should be readable inside the website and downloadable.

## Content Tone

- Warm, faithful, practical, and respectful.
- Avoid careless claims about other religions or civilizations.
- Use Islamic sources carefully and consult scholars for detailed fiqh or hadith grading.
- Speak to the reader personally: “this is for me, my family, my salah, my inner life.”
